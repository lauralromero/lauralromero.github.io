Christian Science Plaza, a

The Christian Science Plaza Project introduced the concept of apertures, and their relationship to architectural spaces. I was tasked with designing a pavilion on the grounds of the Christian Science Plaza that would house two sculptures and also highlight specific parts of the Plaza. The pavilion I created was designed as a path project, through which visitors would be guided by the compression and expansion of spaces created by the pavilion’s walls.
Images created with AutoCAD and Adobe Photoshop.

Museum Project, a

For the Museum Space Project, I was asked to design a museum space with at least two levels, connected by stairs, that housed two portraits from the Museum of Fine Arts in Boston. My museum project's main goal was to architecturally achieve intimate moments, which would reflect the highly intimate nature of the portraits. The idea is to subconsciously guide visitors to these “hidden” spaces, through the deliberate use of apertures, walls, and floor levels.
Images created with AutoCAD and Adobe Illustrator.
